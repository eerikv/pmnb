What is the IPMA certification?

IPMA® is a global leader in project management standards and certifications, with its Competence Baseline (IPMA ICB®) as a comprehensive framework for various project management roles. IPMA®’s Four-Level Certification System is unique worldwide and recognised for its quality to elevating professional standards.
The IPMA 4-L-C System operates with three domains: project, programme and portfolio management; and four levels of competence – Levels A, B, C and D. The IPMA level definitions are constructed against the following core criteria:

  • Level A is constructed in terms of the leadership of others in very complex projects (programmes or portfolios) throughout the life cycle (where applicable) at a strategic level.
  • Level B is constructed in terms of the leadership of others in projects (programmes or portfolios) and includes complexity throughout the life cycle (where applicable).
  • Level C is constructed in terms of the management of others in projects of moderate complexity throughout the life cycle through the application of knowledge/theory across all relevant competences.
  • Level D is constructed in terms of knowledge across all relevant competences only.

IPMA certificate benefits

  • Certification is a popular way for project managers, and members of other similar occupations, to signal their proficiency in the practice of their occupation and for hiring managers to filter job applications.
  • Although certification does not show a strong short-term direct effect on performance, by providing its complementary elements such as professional attitude and networking and its long-term effect such as higher levels of self-efficacy, certification certainly can be a smart move in the career of a project manager and can be useful for the whole occupation of project management.
  • It appears that certification is a good signal of professionalism, which in turn is an indicator of project management self-efficacy, which is our best measure of project manager performance. This three-part relationship may seem to be a relatively circuitous route from certification to performance, as in fact, it is… And certification’s role in signally is especially important for younger professionals as, when we excluded the years of experience, the effect size of certification on both professionalism and self-efficacy tends to be larger.
  • Years of experience show a stronger relationship with professionalism than having a certification. Similarly, it shows a stronger relationship with self-efficacy than professionalism. This verifies the notion that mastery experience is primary source of self-efficacy. Interestingly, years of experience is positively related to strategic performance and not related to operational performance.
  • For human resource professionals, hiring manager and organizations, in the absence of other measures available to winnow down large numbers of applicants, certification may prove a reasonable criterion for this activity when combined with years of PM experience and level of PM training.
  • For the project management practitioner, since certification is likely to continue to be an essential initial screening tool for project management positions, it remains valuable in increasing their success in the market. This result is not necessarily because obtaining it directly indicates PM proficiency, but mainly because the process of obtaining it, joining a professional association, and increasing PM knowledge are all likely to increase self-confidence and professionalism, both of which will likely increase the level of performance.

Differences amongst IPMA Levels

Every level of certification requires knowledges on the competences as described by the Individual Competence Baseline.

IPMA Level A Certification

IPMA level A certification is the highest internationally recognised level as project, programme and/or portfolio professional. You have shown to consistently, over a minimum number of years, apply those competences in a highly complex environment, taking full responsibility in a leading role. You are a professional amongst professionals, recognised by your peers and your customers. You truly deserve your position: project director, programme director or portfolio director.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Candidates must have operated in highly complex project, programme, or portfolio environments with strategic organizational impact.
  • Within the last twelve years, candidates must have a minimum of five years of experience as a leader in very complex projects, programmes, or portfolios.
  • For Programme Director certification, candidates must have five years of experience as a programme manager in a strategic leadership role in very complex programmes, or four years as a programme manager and three years as a project manager in very complex projects at a strategic level.
  • For Portfolio Director certification, candidates must have five years of experience as a portfolio manager in a strategic leadership role in very complex portfolios, or four years as a portfolio manager and three years as a project or programme manager in very complex projects or programmes at a strategic level.

IPMA Level B Certification

Achieving IPMA Level B certification acknowledges your senior expertise in projects, programmes, and portfolios on an international scale. By demonstrating proficiency aligned with the IPMA Individual Competence Baseline® over many years, you've consistently applied these competencies in leading roles, garnering recognition from peers and clients alike. As a true professional, your position as a senior project manager, senior programme manager, or senior portfolio manager is well-deserved.

Entry Criteria for Senior Management Roles
Candidates to have operated within complex organizational environments within the past eight years, candidates must possess a minimum of five years of relevant experience, with at least three years spent in responsible leadership positions:

  • Senior Project Manager: Candidates must have managed complex projects, demonstrating leadership capabilities.
  • Senior Programme Manager: Candidates must have overseen complex programmes, exhibiting strong leadership skills.
  • Senior Portfolio Manager: Candidates must have directed complex portfolios, assuming significant leadership responsibilities.

IPMA Level C Certification

An IPMA level C Certification is an international recognition of your experience level in managing projects. You have shown to consistently apply those, taking responsibility in a leading role. You are a professional. Your clients rely on you to manage their projects in a predictable, professional way.

Entry Criteria for Project Manager

  • The candidate has acted in a project management role within a moderately complex project environment within an organisation.
  • The candidate needs to have a minimum of
    • three years experience as a project manager within projects of moderate complexity, or
    • a minimum of three years experience in a responsible project management role assisting the project manager in complex projects.

IPMA Level D Certification

Achieving IPMA level D certification is international recognition of your knowledge about managing projects.

Entry Criteria for Project Management Associate

  • The candidate has knowledge in the Competence Elements (CEs) related to project management.
  • As such, they usually have broad project management knowledge and may work in a project team.
  • There is no previous experience required and only knowledge regarding CEs related to project management are assessed.

IPMA Perspective

The ”Perspective” section of the IPMA standards can be summarized to 5 main points.

  1. Strategy: Focuses on how strategies are understood and transformed into manageable elements using projects. It highlights the importance of aligning project goals with an organization's mission, vision, and strategy, and ensuring ongoing validity of the business or organizational justification for projects.
  2. Governance Structures and Processes: Deals with the alignment of projects with the established structures, systems, and processes of the organization. It covers knowing and implementing the principles of project, programme, and portfolio management, and aligning the project with supporting functions like human resources, finance, and quality requirements.
  3. Compliance Standards and Regulations: Describes the importance of identifying and ensuring compliance with relevant legislation, health, safety, security, environmental regulations, codes of conduct, sustainability principles, and professional standards.
  4. Power and Interest: Focuses on assessing personal ambitions, informal influence, and working styles of others to benefit the project. It involves understanding the impact of these factors on project success and employing them advantageously.
  5. Culture and Values: Emphasizes assessing the culture and values of the society and organization and their implications for the project. It involves aligning the project with formal and informal cultural values to ensure project success.
